About

OncoOmics is publicly available portal that includes multi-omics data for 10 Cancer types of TCGA and NCI projects and 10 Clinical Proteomics Tumor Analysis Consortium (CPTAC) cancer cohorts. The web application has analytical modules which contain omics data for OncoOmics Finder, OncoOmics Interpreter, OncoOmics Visulization and OncoOmics Prediction for prognosis or predictive models. OncoOmics Finder allows users to search for attributes that are associated with a query attribute, such as gene, drug, mRNA or protein expression signatures of genomic alterations, candidate biomarkers of clinical attributes, SNPs, driver genes, cell line for cancer types. Analysis results can be visualized by scatter plots, box plots, or Kaplan-Meier plots, heatmap. To derive biological insights from the association results, the OncoOmics Interpreter module performs enrichment analysis based on Gene ontology, onco pathways, network modules (gene-drug and protein-protein). The OncoOmics visulization module uses data visualization functions (interactive venn diagram, scatter plot, and sortable heat map) and meta-analysis to compare and integrate association results generated by the OncoOmics Finder module, which supports multi-omics analysis in a 10-cancer type. OncoOmics Prediction allows users to extract the prognosis or predictive models for 10 cancer types against driver genes. OncoOmics provides a unique platform for biologists and clinicians to access, analyze and compare cancer multi-omics data within and across tumor types.
